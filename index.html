Hey there, I'm Alex.

I'm an M.Sc. candidate at the University of Denver studying global change effects in high elevation forests. I am specifically interested in the localized morphological and physiological adjustments that dominant tree species make in response to adverse shifts in temperature and precipitation regimes, and the downstream impacts those adjustments have on population and community processses. The increasing frequency and intensity of monsoon-failure summer droughts in the southern Rocky Mountains of Colorado and New Mexico provides an ideal system to study these types of local adaptation patterns and the consequeunces extreme droughts have on landscape structure and functioning in montane ecosystems.

I also contribute to ongoing work at Washington State University Tree Fruit Research and Extension Center specializing in tree phsyiology, orchard management, and evaluations of new technologies for field, harvest, and packing line improvements. Most notably, I have worked on developing recommendations for management of new WSU variety Cosmic Crisp to be released in the Fall of 2019 and have been testing near-infrared spectroscopy for applications in non-destructive fruit quality evaluation.

I believe science as a discipline, and particularily ecology and global change biology, can only advance through solution-driven investigations paired with effective communication and community outreach. As such, I prioitize making meaningful connections with the broader communities impacted by my work through partnerships with local agencies while putting forth every effort I can in mentoring young scientists and advocating for LGBTQIA visibility in STEM fields.
